He had come, that day, to sleep at the Bastille. The great chamber five toises[64] square, which he had at the Louvre, with its huge chimney-piece loaded with twelve great beasts and thirteen great prophets, and his grand bed, eleven feet by twelve, pleased him but little. He felt himself lost amid all this grandeur. This good _bourgeois_ king preferred the Bastille with a tiny chamber and couch. And then, the Bastille was stronger than the Louvre.
