The greater part of these privileges, it may be noted in passing, and there were some even better than the above, had been extorted from the kings by revolts and mutinies. It is the course of things from time immemorial; the king only lets go when the people tear away. There is an old charter which puts the matter naively: _àpropos_ of fidelity: _Civibus fidelitas in reges, quæ tamen aliquoties seditionibus interrupta, multa peperit privilegia_.
