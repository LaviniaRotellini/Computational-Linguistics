The Friends of the A B C were not numerous, it was a secret society in the state of embryo, we might almost say a coterie, if coteries ended in heroes. They assembled in Paris in two localities, near the fish-market, in a wine-shop called _Corinthe_, of which more will be heard later on, and near the Pantheon in a little café in the Rue Saint-Michel called the _Café Musain_, now torn down; the first of these meeting-places was close to the workingman, the second to the students.
