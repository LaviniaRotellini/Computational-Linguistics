He had passed Lillois and Bois-Seigneur-Isaac. In the west he perceived the slate-roofed tower of Braine-l’Alleud, which has the form of a reversed vase. He had just left behind a wood upon an eminence; and at the angle of the crossroad, by the side of a sort of mouldy gibbet bearing the inscription _Ancient Barrier No. 4_, a public house, bearing on its front this sign: _At the Four Winds_ (Aux Quatre Vents). _Échabeau, Private Café_.
