The uncle and the niece were at all events agreed in not being in haste to seek a solution of these problems. When Déruchette began to be regarded as a probable heiress, a crowd of suitors had presented themselves. Attentions under these circumstances are not generally worth much. Mess Lethierry felt this. He would grumble out the old French proverb, "_A maiden of gold, a suitor of brass_." He politely showed the fortune-seekers to the door. He was content to wait, and so was Déruchette.
